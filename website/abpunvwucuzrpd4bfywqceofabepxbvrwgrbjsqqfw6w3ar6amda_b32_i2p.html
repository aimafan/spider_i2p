3. communication

how would entities who are aware of the digital nature of the mind attempt to communicate with humans? to determine an answer to this question, one can synthesize the answers to two other questions. the first question is, how have humans begun to communicate with each other in an increasingly digitized world? the second question is, how would such entities communicate with each other?

as this world becomes increasingly digitized, new methods of communication that do not depend on physical or temporal co-presence have become very popular. modern digital communication methods have made it possible to communicate in circumstances which would otherwise render it impossible to communicate at all, and they have also made it possible to communicate many kinds of information more quickly and easily than ever before.

the radio allowed average people to hear others without requiring physical co-presence. the telephone allowed average people to speak to others without requiring physical co-presence. the television allowed average people to see others without requiring physical co-presence. video telephony allowed average people to be seen by others without requiring physical co-presence.

as technologies which reduced the necessity of physical co-presence were developed, so were technologies which reduced the necessity of temporal co-presence. radio and television shows could be recorded and played back later. voicemail could be left for the intended recipient of a phone call if they couldn't answer it at the time. and there have been a variety of services to which people been uploading short video messages for several years now.

there is one limitation shared by all of these communication methods which is virtually always overlooked: the requirement to use specific physical objects which act as communication interfaces. today, smartphones are the most commonly used interfaces for transceiving information of all kinds, their versatility due to the range of software they can run. but communication would be more convenient and more versatile still if the requirement to use a physical object to transceive information could be obviated entirely.

this brings us to the next question: how would entities who are aware of the digital nature of the mind communicate with each other?
